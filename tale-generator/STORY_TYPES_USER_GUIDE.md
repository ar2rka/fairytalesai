# Story Types User Guide

## Overview

The Tale Generator now supports **three different types of bedtime stories** to provide more variety and engagement for children. Each story type offers a unique narrative approach while maintaining the same high-quality, personalized content.

## Story Types

### 1. Child Story üëßüë¶

**What it is:** A story where your child is the main character and protagonist.

**Best for:**
- Building self-confidence
- Personalizing moral lessons
- Making your child feel special and important
- Bedtime stories focused on the child's interests

**Example scenario:**
"Emma discovers a magical garden where she learns about the importance of kindness..."

**How to use:**
1. Select "Child Story" as the story type
2. Choose your child's profile
3. Select language and moral value
4. Generate!

---

### 2. Hero Story ü¶∏

**What it is:** A story featuring a heroic character as the main protagonist.

**Best for:**
- Introducing role models
- Teaching through heroic examples
- Diverse character representation
- Stories with adventure and action

**Example scenario:**
"Captain Wonder embarks on a quest to save the Crystal Kingdom, learning that true bravery means helping others..."

**How to use:**
1. Select "Hero Story" as the story type
2. Choose your child's profile (for age-appropriate content)
3. **Select a hero character** (must match story language)
4. Choose moral value and length
5. Generate!

**Important:** The hero you select must be available in the same language as your story (English or Russian).

---

### 3. Combined Adventure üåü

**What it is:** A collaborative story where your child and a hero character work together.

**Best for:**
- Teaching teamwork and cooperation
- Mentor-mentee relationship stories
- Extended adventures (typically longer)
- Combining personal connection with heroic inspiration

**Example scenario:**
"Emma meets the legendary Luna the Starkeeper, and together they embark on a journey through the constellation realm to restore the night sky..."

**How to use:**
1. Select "Combined Adventure" as the story type
2. Choose your child's profile
3. **Select a hero character** (must match story language)
4. Choose moral value and length
5. Generate!

**Special feature:** Combined stories automatically generate a relationship description (e.g., "Emma meets the legendary Captain Wonder") that sets the context for their adventure together.

## Choosing the Right Story Type

### Decision Guide

| Situation | Recommended Type |
|-----------|------------------|
| First-time using the app | **Child Story** - easiest and most personal |
| Child loves adventure stories | **Hero Story** or **Combined** |
| Teaching teamwork | **Combined Adventure** |
| Longer bedtime reading | **Combined Adventure** (7-10 minutes) |
| Building self-esteem | **Child Story** |
| Introducing role models | **Hero Story** |
| Child has specific hero they love | **Hero Story** or **Combined** |

### Age Recommendations

- **Ages 3-5:** Child stories work best (simpler narratives)
- **Ages 6-8:** All types work well
- **Ages 9-12:** Hero and Combined stories often more engaging

## Tips for Best Results

### 1. Match Story Type to Mood
- **Calm bedtime:** Child stories with gentle morals
- **Energetic time:** Hero or Combined stories
- **Learning moment:** Combined stories with clear lessons

### 2. Vary Story Types
- Don't use the same type every night
- Rotate between types to keep interest high
- Track which types your child enjoys most

### 3. Discuss After Reading
- Ask your child about the characters
- Discuss the moral lesson
- For combined stories, talk about teamwork
- For hero stories, discuss heroic qualities

### 4. Create Custom Heroes
- Design heroes based on your child's interests
- Create family members as heroes
- Make heroes that reflect your cultural values

### 5. Language Consistency
- Stick with one language per story
- Use Russian stories to support language learning
- Heroes and child profiles can be language-specific

## Troubleshooting

### "No heroes available for this language"

**Problem:** You selected a story type requiring a hero, but no heroes exist in your chosen language.

**Solution:**
1. Click "Create a Hero" button in the alert
2. Or switch to a language that has heroes
3. Or contact support to add more heroes

### "Hero language mismatch"

**Problem:** The selected hero's language doesn't match the story language.

**Solution:** Change either the story language or select a different hero that matches.

### "Hero selection not appearing"

**Problem:** You can't see the hero selector.

**Solution:** Make sure you've selected either "Hero Story" or "Combined Adventure" as the story type. The hero selector only appears for these types.
